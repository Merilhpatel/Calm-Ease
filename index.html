Understanding Depression

Depression (major depressive disorder) is a common and serious medical illness that negatively affects how you feel, the way you think and how you act. Fortunately, it is also treatable. Depression causes feelings of sadness and/or a loss of interest in activities you once enjoyed.

The Impact of Depression

Tiredness and poor concentration are common. Depression is a leading cause of disability around the world and contributes greatly to the global burden of disease. The effects of depression can be long-lasting or recurrent and can dramatically affect a person's ability to function and live a rewarding life.

Breaking the Stigma

Breaking the stigma of depression is crucial for fostering a society where individuals feel safe and encouraged to seek help and support. It involves normalizing conversations around mental health, promoting empathy, and emphasizing that depression is an illness, not a personal weakness. By openly discussing depression and providing education, we can create an environment that empowers those affected to reach out for assistance without fear of judgment, ultimately leading to better mental health outcomes for all.
